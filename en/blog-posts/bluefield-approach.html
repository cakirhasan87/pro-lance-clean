In a greenfield implementation, a new ERP system is implemented from scratch, often in organizations that do not have an existing ERP system.

Conversely, a brownfield implementation involves upgrading or replacing an existing ERP system without major changes to the business processes.

Bluefield implementation allows organizations to take advantage of the benefits of a new ERP system while leveraging existing business processes and data.

This approach is particularly useful when the current ERP system is outdated, inefficient, or does not meet the organization's evolving needs.
